In the particular, lies the universal.‎

Be authentically you.‎

October 10, 2017

Everyone has a story. So, I guess I should share mine?

Instead of writing some bullshit, it's time to get personal. Letting go of your insecurities and being vulnerable will connect you to your audience.

...

So let's get personal.

...

When I was younger, I thought I was cool. I thought I was cooler than everyone. I've had many friends who I've basically abandoned for popularity or other social purposes. Sometimes I felt like I never had a choice but looking back you know you could have done something differently.

I'm sorry homies.

And now here I am reflecting on all the friendships abandoned, while I'm alone at a conference with +10,000 people.

To be honest, maybe I would have never changed. But of course everything comes full circle and I had the same thing happen to me. In college, I hung out with another group of dudes that I thought were cool. I basically kicked it with these guys everyday; partying, playing fifa, sharing songs on soundcloud.

I felt at home.

Then one day, one of the guys I thought was my friend decides that I'm not allowed to hang out at his place anymore. And just like that, all the friends who I thought were my friends, weren't.

...

Sometimes humans are mean. And sometimes the more you get hurt, the more you want to shell away.

...

After a couple weeks, I stopped feeling sorry for myself. The image you portray will attract a certain type of audience. I was portraying myself as a certain type of "cool". The "cool" who tries to be cooler than you. The "cool" who shits on losers. The "cool" guy whose life looks so "cool".

In middle school and high school, I thought that was what I was supposed to be. I thought that being cool meant I had to get a nice car, get a cool job, get a fake ID, be able to drink a lot, be able to smoke a lot, get party connections, party super crazy; basically everything that all the "cool" kids were screaming at me to do.

But that's not me. Now I'm the guy writing about a particular moment, hoping to shed some light.

...

In the particular, lies the universal.
