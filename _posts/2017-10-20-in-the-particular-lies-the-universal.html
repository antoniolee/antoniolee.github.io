In the particular, lies the universal.‎

Be authentically you.‎

October 10, 2017

Everyone has a story. So, I guess I should share mine?

Instead of writing some bullshit, it's time to get personal. Letting go of your insecurities and being vulnerable will connect you to your audience.

...

So let's get personal.

...

When I was younger, I thought I was cool. I thought I was cooler than everyone. I've had many friends who I've basically abandoned for popularity or other social purposes.

I'm sorry guys.

And now here I am reflecting on all the friendships abandoned, while I'm alone at a conference with +10,000 people.

To be honest, I probably would have never changed. But I had the same thing happen to me. In college, I hung out with other dudes that I thought were cool. I basically kicked it with these guys everyday; smoking weed, playing fifa, sharing songs on soundcloud.

I felt at home.

Than one day, one of the guys I thought was my friend decides that I'm not allowed to hang out at his place anymore. And in an instant, all the friends who I thought were my friends, weren't. I was outcasted.

...

Humans are mean. And the more you get hurt, the more you want to shell away.

...

After a couple weeks, I stopped feeling sorry for myself, and decided to change. The image you portray will attract a certain type of audience. I was portraying myself as a certain type of "cool". The "cool" who is cooler than you. The "cool" who shits on losers. The "cool" whose life looks so fucking "cool".

In middle school and high school, I thought that was what I was supposed to be. I thought I had to have a hot girlfriend, get a nice car, get a cool job, get a fake ID, be able to drink a lot, be able to smoke a lot, get drug connections, get party connections, party super crazy; basically everything that all the "cool" kids were screaming at me to do.

But that's not me. Now I'm the guy writing about a particular moment, hoping to shed some light. Because I know that I'm not the only one.

...

In the particular, lies the universal.
